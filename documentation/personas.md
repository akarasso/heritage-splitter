# Personas

## Persona 1: Leo — The Artist

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Leo |
| **Age** | 35 years old |
| **Occupation** | Digital & physical artist |
| **Location** | France |

### Artist Experience
Experienced, accustomed to multi-artist collaborations. Advanced NFT experience: mints his own works, has a detailed understanding of encryption keys, wallets, and royalty distribution mechanisms.

### Objectives (Top 3)
1. **Protect intellectual property upstream** — Mint concepts or ideas with an encryption key as proof of prior art.
2. **Mint 100% independently** — Publish any file format in just a few clicks, without the need for intermediaries.
3. **Collaborate easily** — Integrate the wallets of all co-creators from the outset of the project to secure everyone's shares.

### Frustrations
- Manual management of the primary sale: having to receive 100% of the funds and manually redistribute shares to collaborators.
- Promotional workload and "Open Calls": the frustration of having to respond to calls for projects and time wasted on social media communication instead of creating.

### Major Concerns
- Theft of intellectual concepts prior to production (need to track original ideas).
- Fair compensation for contributors without administrative friction during sales.
- Ensuring the traceability of physical works associated with an NFT.

### Priority Needs
- **Intellectual property**: A system that acts as an "NFT" to protect non-public documents with a "stamp" status before validation by all participants.
- **Collaboration**: Validation of contracts and distribution by sending a message to the network and having everyone validate before minting.
- **Visibility**: Ability to promote his work quickly and efficiently via showrooms.

## Persona 2: Gil — The Art Producer

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Gil |
| **Age** | 51 years old |
| **Occupation** | Producer & physical/digital gallery owner |
| **Location** | France |

### Artist Experience
Artistic experience: has owned rights to works since the 1990s. NFT experience: has already carried out drops (e.g., collection of several thousand works), with successful sales via collector networks.

### Objectives (Top 3)
1. **Create heritage and community value** — By exhibiting digital works in physical installations or through quests to increase the value of the collection.
2. **Manage rights transfer agreements legally** — By redistributing percentages of sales to the original creators of the images it produces.
3. **Facilitate gallery purchases** — Allow visitors to instantly purchase an NFT (digital or physical) without complex technical friction.

### Frustrations
- The inadequacy of mainstream platforms: OpenSea and similar platforms are not designed for physical galleries that want to exhibit digital artists for a few months and sell in a hybrid manner.
- Costs and legal complexity: had to spend a fortune on legal fees to create a smart contract that strictly complies with copyright law (immutable resale right).
- Avoiding complexity for the end buyer in the gallery.

### Major Concerns
- Strict compliance with the law: ensuring that no artist (even on the other side of the world) is financially disadvantaged by resales.
- Long-term appreciation of the collection through exclusivity and controlling scarcity (the "Bitcoin" model).

### Priority Needs
- **Intellectual property**: A tool that automates the execution of rights transfer agreements for Web3, without the need for specialized lawyers.
- **Creation tools**: Ability to create his own showcase featuring the works of the artists he represents at a given moment on his website.
- **Collaboration**: Ability to connect buyers interested in an exhibited work with the collection who owns the NFT, acting as a "matchmaker".
